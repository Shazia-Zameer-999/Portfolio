{# ── Story column ──────────────────────────────────────── #}

I've always been curious about how things work beneath the surface. Whether it's a web application, a browser rendering issue, or even something outside software, I naturally find myself asking "Why does this work this way?" That curiosity is what first pulled me into programming, and it's still what keeps me excited about engineering today.

I'm currently pursuing a B.Tech in Computer Science, but most of my learning has come from building. From freelancing for clients to developing personal projects, I've learned that real growth happens when you leave tutorials behind, face unfamiliar problems, and patiently work through them until they finally make sense.

I don't enjoy building projects just to add another line to my resume. I enjoy creating things that solve real problems while teaching me something new in the process. Every project, every bug, and every challenge is another opportunity to think more deeply, write better code, and become a better engineer than I was yesterday.
